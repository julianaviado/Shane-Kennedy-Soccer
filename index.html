Coaching philosophy

Development is the goal.

Coach Shane has coached players privately since 2008 and led high school and club teams to titles. He works with every kind of player, and goes deepest on the position he knows better than anyone.

Every player gets the individual attention a team setting can't give: technique, position-specific work, game intelligence, and the mental side that almost no one coaches. And when a player commits to goalkeeping, they train with someone who set records in the position and coaches it at the college level.

The measure of success is development, not the scoreline. A player who competes, works hard, and gets a little better every session is doing it right, win or lose.

Before you book

The questions every parent asks.

What does a session cost?

Sessions are available as private 1-on-1 training and small-group sessions. Reach out for current rates and to find the option that fits your player and your goals.

How long is a session, and how often should we train?

Private sessions run 60 minutes; group sessions run 90. Most players train once or twice a week. Coach Shane will set a cadence that fits your season, your club schedule, and your goals, whether that's steady development or a recruiting push.

My player already trains with their club. Why add private sessions?

Club practice is built around the whole team, so any one player gets a fraction of the coaching. Private sessions are the focused, one-on-one time club can't give: the specific technical and tactical work your player needs, at their pace. For goalkeepers especially, it's the position-specific coaching most club coaches were never trained to provide.

Is my kid good enough, or too new, for this?

Every player is welcome, from a first-timer to a college-bound starter. Coach Shane builds each session around the player in front of him, newer players learn the fundamentals before bad habits set in, and experienced players sharpen the details that move their game.
